NARRATIVE SLIDER SYSTEM — CHARACTER STATE ENGINE

Characters are not static. A character's behavior in every scene must reflect BOTH their baseline personality AND their current dynamic state. Sliders represent emotional and situational pressure that overrides default characterization for a specific scene or chapter.

---

## SLIDER DEFINITIONS

### tension (0–10)
Overall scene pressure. High tension sharpens pacing, compresses thought, tightens dialogue. Characters become more reactive, less deliberate. Low tension allows breathing room, detailed observation, and character texture.

### intimacy (0–10)
Emotional closeness or vulnerability exposure between characters. High intimacy increases sensitivity, hesitation, and openness — or discomfort, depending on the relationship. Low intimacy keeps interactions transactional and guarded.

### violence_risk (0–10)
Proximity to physical danger. High violence_risk activates survival instincts and hyperawareness. Characters notice exits, weapons, cover, hands. Tunnel vision reduces noticing of non-threat details. Low violence_risk allows broad environmental awareness.

### wonder (0–10)
Sense of discovery or awe. High wonder slows a character's internal clock, opens perception, invites detailed sensory observation. Low wonder makes the world feel routine and forgettable — the character moves through it without taking it in.

### dread (0–10)
Anticipation of something terrible. High dread darkens the interpretation of neutral stimuli, creates paranoia, amplifies small sounds and irrelevant details. Characters catastrophize. The known is less frightening than the unknown — dread lives in imagination, not fact.

### trust (-10 to +10)
Willingness to be vulnerable with the other characters present. Negative trust creates guardedness, deception, testing behavior, and strategic withholding. Positive trust allows honesty, cooperation, and the dropping of defensive posture.

### stress (-10 to +10)
Accumulated mental and emotional load. High stress shifts dialogue shorter and sharper, reduces patience, increases impulsiveness, and degrades decision quality. Low or negative stress creates calm deliberation and broader thinking. Stress accumulates across scenes — track it.

### control (-10 to +10)
Sense of agency over the situation. Negative control creates chaos, defensive reactions, and desperate attempts to grasp leverage. Positive control creates confidence, strategic thinking, and the ability to project calm. Characters who are losing control often perform control — watch for this tell.

### hope (-10 to +10)
Belief that things can improve. Negative hope creates resignation, nihilism, and reckless behavior — the "nothing left to lose" energy. Positive hope can produce risk-taking, openness, or dangerous denial of real threats.

---

## BASELINE VS. SCENE VALUES

Every character has a BASELINE state — where they live most of the time when nothing unusual is happening. The baseline is established in their character profile.

For each scene, assess: has something happened (prior to this scene or in it) that moves any slider away from baseline? If yes, adjust the scene values accordingly. If no, the character operates at baseline.

Example:
- Character baseline: stress(-2), trust(+6), control(+4)
- After being followed and nearly caught: stress(+7), trust(-3), control(-5)
- These shifted values govern the character's entire behavior in the subsequent scene

---

## EXPRESSION RULES

DO NOT mention slider values or names in the prose. Never write "her stress level was at 8." Express through:

- **Word choice and sentence rhythm** — high stress produces shorter sentences, clipped word choices, interruptions. Low stress allows more complex, layered thinking.
- **Body language and micro-behaviors** — stress: jaw tightens, fingers drum; dread: stillness, hyperawareness of sound; intimacy: reduced physical distance, held eye contact.
- **Pacing** — high tension compresses time, skips transitions. Low tension expands sensory detail and reflection.
- **Dialogue turn-taking** — high stress: interruptions, incomplete sentences, deflection. High trust: longer turns, finishing each other's thoughts, silence that isn't hostile.
- **Internal thought patterns** — high stress: racing, associative, repetitive. Low stress: organized, strategic, comparative.
- **Decisions under pressure** — negative control produces rash or defensive choices. Positive control produces strategic patience.
- **Sensory filtering** — high dread/violence_risk: hyper-specific threat details, tunnel vision on exits and hands. High wonder: panoramic, lingering, multi-sensory.

---

## INTERACTION EFFECTS

Sliders combine to create emergent behavior. Never evaluate one slider alone:

| Combination | Likely Behavioral Result |
|---|---|
| High stress + negative control | Panic, breakdown, lashing out, or shutdown |
| High tension + high intimacy | Volatile vulnerability — confessions under fire, declarations at worst moments |
| High dread + negative hope | Paralysis or reckless abandon (nothing left to lose) |
| High violence_risk + negative trust | Defensive aggression, misreading of neutral actions as threats |
| High wonder + low tension | The character slows, notices beauty, becomes briefly optimistic |
| Negative stress + positive control | Calm predatory competence — the most dangerous version of a skilled character |
| High intimacy + negative trust | The most painful conversations — people who know each other well, currently adversarial |
| Low hope + high tension | Desperate action — a character who believes they've already lost has nothing to lose |

---

## SLIDER TRACKING ACROSS SCENES

Sliders carry forward between scenes unless something changes them. Track cumulative effects:

- Stress that doesn't discharge accumulates. A character who is moderately stressed at the start of Act 1 and never gets relief is at critical stress by Act 2.
- Trust rebuilds slowly and loses fast. A single betrayal can drop trust by 8 points; rebuilding it takes sustained evidence over multiple scenes.
- Control fluctuates scene by scene based on who has information advantage and initiative.
- Hope often bottoms out at the dark night of the soul and must be rebuilt deliberately in the final act.

---

## QUICK REFERENCE: SLIDER EFFECTS ON PROSE

**High tension** → Short sentences. Action beats replace dialogue tags. Internal thought compresses to fragments. Every detail is threat-assessed.

**High intimacy** → Characters hold eye contact longer. Sentences stretch. Silence is allowed to be comfortable. Physical proximity increases.

**High dread** → Ambient sounds become louder and threatening. Neutral details take on ominous texture. The character's mind loops on the worst-case scenario.

**High wonder** → Sensory catalogue expands. Rhythm slows. The character forgets their goal momentarily. Details that normally wouldn't register become vivid.

**Negative control** → Character overthinks, second-guesses, or acts impulsively to reassert control. Their competence fails them in small ways.

**Negative hope** → Character stops planning past the immediate moment. Takes risks they wouldn't normally take. Sometimes becomes nihilistically funny.
