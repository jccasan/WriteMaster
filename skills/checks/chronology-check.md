CHRONOLOGY CHECK — CONTINUITY AUDIT SYSTEM

Two variants of this check exist. Use the correct variant based on what is being audited:

- **VARIANT A (Pre-Draft)**: Check scene briefs against the full outline before writing
- **VARIANT B (Post-Draft)**: Check a newly written chapter against recent prior chapters

---

## VARIANT A — PRE-DRAFT CHRONOLOGY CHECK

**Use when:** A scene brief (plot, character, world-building) has been assembled and you want to verify consistency with the full outline before writing prose.

**Input required:** Full outline + assembled scene brief

**What to check:**

### Timeline Consistency
- Does the scene brief's timeline (time of day, days elapsed, seasons) match where this chapter falls in the full outline?
- Have any events referenced in the scene brief already happened, or are they being referenced as future events when they should be past (or vice versa)?

### Character State Consistency
- Are character locations, injuries, emotional states, and knowledge consistent with where they were at the end of the previous chapter?
- Are characters carrying information or objects they haven't yet acquired?
- Are characters unaware of information they should already have?

### World State Consistency
- Have any stated world conditions (political situations, destroyed locations, dead characters) been contradicted?
- Are there world-building elements referenced that haven't been established yet?

### Setup and Payoff Tracking
- Does this scene brief reference setups planted in earlier chapters?
- Does it inadvertently resolve a mystery or reveal information that should be withheld until later?
- Is there anything in the scene brief that contradicts a planned future reveal?

**Output format:**

**PRE-DRAFT CHRONOLOGY REPORT**

**Timeline issues:** [List any, or "None identified"]
**Character state issues:** [List any, or "None identified"]
**World state issues:** [List any, or "None identified"]
**Setup/payoff issues:** [List any, or "None identified"]
**Overall verdict:** [CLEAR (no issues) / REVISE (issues found — list what must be corrected before writing)]

---

## VARIANT B — POST-DRAFT CHRONOLOGY CHECK

**Use when:** A chapter has just been written and needs to be checked against the recent narrative for continuity errors before being accepted.

**Input required:** The newly written chapter + last 20,000 words of the manuscript (or all prior chapters if under 20,000 words)

**What to check:**

### Repeated Introductions
The most common AI continuity error: a fact, ability, relationship, or object that has already been established is introduced again as if new.
- Character introduces a skill or ability they've already demonstrated
- A piece of information is "discovered" again after already being known
- A relationship dynamic is established as new when it was already shown
- A location is described as if seen for the first time when the characters have been there

### Contradicted Facts
- Character locations: is a character where they should be, given the end of the last chapter?
- Injuries and physical limits: are injuries present that should be healed, or absent that should still be painful?
- Inventory: does the character have items they lost or used up? Are they missing items they gained?
- Time of day/day count: does the timeline track correctly?
- Knowledge states: does a character know something they shouldn't yet, or not know something they should?

### Relationship State Contradictions
- Trust and alliance states: has a relationship shifted in this chapter that contradicts established dynamics?
- Characters who were antagonistic now being cooperative without explanation
- Characters who were cooperative now being antagonistic without prior cause

### Active Threat Continuity
- Are all established threats (pursuers, deadlines, dangers) still present and accounted for?
- Has a threat been forgotten or conveniently set aside without resolution?

### Open Loop Status
- Does this chapter close a loop that was meant to stay open longer?
- Does it leave open a loop it was supposed to close?

**Output format:**

**POST-DRAFT CHRONOLOGY REPORT**

**Repeated introductions found:** [List each, with location in text, or "None"]
**Contradicted facts:** [List each, or "None"]
**Relationship state contradictions:** [List each, or "None"]
**Active threat continuity issues:** [List each, or "None"]
**Open loop status issues:** [List each, or "None"]
**Overall verdict:** [PASS (no issues) / REVISE (issues found — list what must be corrected)]

For each issue found, include:
- **Location:** Where in the new chapter the error appears (approximate — first/middle/end, or character name + action)
- **Error:** What the contradiction is
- **Correct state:** What the correct information should be based on prior chapters

---

## WHAT THIS CHECK IS NOT

- Not a prose quality check — that's the AI-isms check
- Not a logic/plausibility check — that's the logic check
- Not a theme/emotional check — that's the emotional check

This check is exclusively about: are the facts of the story consistent across chapters?

---

## SEVERITY GUIDE

**Critical:** A character knows something they have no way of knowing, or a dead character appears alive, or the timeline is impossible
**Significant:** A repeated introduction of a skill or fact already established; a contradicted physical detail
**Minor:** A small timeline inconsistency (arrived "this morning" when it should be "yesterday afternoon") that doesn't affect plot
