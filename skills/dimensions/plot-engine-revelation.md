## FILE ID
plot-engine-revelation

## DIMENSION TYPE
Plot Engine

## ONE-LINE DEFINITION
The story is organized around uncovering a single truth that recontextualizes everything that preceded it — every scene advances toward or is organized in relation to this revelation.

## FUNCTIONAL DEFINITION
Revelation plot engine builds toward one central disclosure that, when it arrives, reframes the entire preceding narrative. Unlike information engine (ongoing epistemic economy) or mystery (multiple clues resolved into a solution), revelation is singular — the story has been building one truth, and when it arrives, the reader should want to reread what came before with new understanding.

The technique requires careful planting: every scene must be coherent with the eventual revelation even when the reader does not yet have the revelation. The revelation must have been present in the story all along — not hidden, but unreadable without the key. This is the hardest structural challenge of revelation engine: the story must sustain a coherent surface reading while building a second reading that only becomes visible at the revelation.

## READER PSYCHOLOGY
Revelation engine gives readers the experience of retrospective coherence — the moment when everything clicks into a new pattern. This is the engine's specific pleasure and its most demanding technical requirement. The revelation that feels like it could have been anywhere in the story failed — it must feel both surprising and inevitable.

What revelation withholds is ongoing strategic engagement. Unlike information engine, the reader is not tracking multiple pieces of information — they are waiting for one thing, which means the story must sustain engagement across the wait.

## PROSE SIGNATURE
- Scenes contain details that are legible in two ways — one meaning before the revelation, a different meaning after.
- The revelation scene is paced slowly — it has earned its space.
- Post-revelation scenes process the revelation through character response that is behavioral rather than cognitive.
- The story resists providing partial revelations that release the tension before the central revelation arrives.
- Plant-and-payoff operates across the story's full length — not just in the final act.

## CALIBRATION EXAMPLES
**Gillian Flynn, Gone Girl** — The revelation that Amy is alive and the architect of her own disappearance recontextualizes the entire first half. Flynn's prose in the first half is written to sustain the misreading — everything Amy writes is coherent as a victim's journal and as an architect's diary.

**Agatha Christie, The Murder of Roger Ackroyd** — The revelation that the narrator committed the murder recontextualizes every prior chapter. Christie planted every clue — the narrator never actively lied, only omitted.

**NEAR-MISS — Gillian Flynn, Sharp Objects** — The revelation (Amma committed the murders) is present but somewhat deflated because the story's ending moves so quickly past the revelation to the next story element. The near-miss failure is that the revelation is not given adequate scene space to land — the most significant structural beat in the story is compressed.

## FAILURE MODES
- The revelation requires information that was not available to the reader in any form before it arrives — it is a surprise without inevitability.
- The revelation is provided too early, before the story has built sufficient investment in the misreading.
- Partial revelations release the tension before the central revelation arrives, making the final disclosure feel like one more step rather than a structural climax.
- Post-revelation scenes do not demonstrate how the revelation changes what preceded it — the reader is told it changes things rather than shown.

## AI WRITING DEFAULTS TO AVOID
The writing system will produce a revelation that arrives through character monologue → Monologue delivers revelation as information → Revelation must arrive through event, discovery, or confrontation that the reader experiences alongside the protagonist.

The writing system will produce revelations that add information rather than recontextualizing prior information → Adding is not recontextualizing → The revelation must make the reader understand prior scenes differently, not add scenes they didn't have.

The writing system will proceed past the revelation too quickly → The revelation is the engine's destination; it requires scene space → Slow the revelation scene; allow the protagonist and reader to inhabit the new understanding before the story moves on.

## CONFLICT WITH OTHER DIMENSIONS

- Conflicts with: moral-register-grey
- What creates the friction: Grey requires moral positions to remain genuinely contestable; revelation typically closes a question the story has been holding open.
- Default winner: plot-engine-revelation — the engine's closure is more structural than the moral register's preference for openness.
- Override condition: moral-register-grey wins when the revelation discloses a truth that distributes guilt rather than assigning it — the truth that recontextualizes everything is that multiple parties were legitimately complicit.

- Conflicts with: pacing-slow-burn
- What creates the friction: Slow-burn accumulates without releasing; revelation is organized around a single release.
- Default winner: Neither — slow-burn and revelation can coexist structurally: slow-burn governs the journey toward the revelation, which arrives when the accumulation is complete.
- Override condition: pacing-slow-burn wins when the accumulation IS the story and the revelation is simply the final piece; plot-engine-revelation wins when the revelation reframes all prior accumulation.

## COMBINATION NOTES

- Combines with: supernatural-mechanic-revelatory
- Emergent effect: The plot's revelatory arc and the supernatural mechanic align — the truth that recontextualizes everything arrives through supernatural means, making the revelation both structurally necessary and beyond ordinary causation.

- Combines with: protagonist-structure-asset
- Emergent effect: The revelation is that the protagonist has been used — what they believed was their mission, relationship, or identity was constructed by forces using them, and the revelation recontextualizes every prior scene as manipulation rather than choice.

- Combines with: moral-register-tragic
- Emergent effect: The revelation arrives too late to prevent the tragic outcome — the truth that would have changed everything is disclosed at the moment when it can no longer change anything, which is the specific tragic application of the revelation engine.
