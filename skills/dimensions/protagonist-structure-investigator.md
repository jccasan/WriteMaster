## FILE ID
protagonist-structure-investigator

## DIMENSION TYPE
Protagonist Structure

## ONE-LINE DEFINITION
Someone whose mode of engaging with the world is inquiry — they solve problems by knowing more than others, and the investigation is their primary form of action.

## FUNCTIONAL DEFINITION
Investigator protagonist structure makes the act of inquiry the character's primary identity. The investigator does not act first — they observe, gather, analyze, and then act when they know enough. Their competence is epistemological: they are good at learning what others don't want them to know. This makes them specifically suited to information and mystery plot engines but also capable of driving other engines through their investigative methodology.

The investigator structure requires that the methodology be specific and visible. A generic "smart detective who figures things out" is not an investigator protagonist — a protagonist whose specific way of gathering and processing information is the story's subject is. The reader must be able to understand and follow the investigator's reasoning process, not just receive its conclusions.

## PROSE SIGNATURE
- Observation scenes are specific — the investigator notices particular details, not generally everything.
- The investigator's reasoning process is shown in steps the reader can follow rather than delivered as conclusions.
- Wrong hypotheses are demonstrated before being discarded — the investigator's errors are part of the methodology.
- Social interactions are investigative — every conversation is a data-gathering exercise.

## CALIBRATION EXAMPLES
**Arthur Conan Doyle, Sherlock Holmes stories** — The investigative methodology is the story's subject. Holmes's reasoning is shown in specific steps visible to the reader.

**Tana French, In the Woods** — The investigation is Rob Ryan's form of self-understanding as much as crime-solving — the investigative structure reveals the investigator's psychology through its methodology.

**NEAR-MISS — Stieg Larsson, The Girl with the Dragon Tattoo (Blomkvist)** — Blomkvist's investigation is competent but his methodology is not distinctive — he is a good journalist investigating in the way good journalists investigate. The near-miss failure is that his investigative approach could be any competent investigator's, making the structure operative rather than genuinely investigative.

## FAILURE MODES
- The investigator's conclusions arrive without visible reasoning process — they know things without the reader seeing how.
- The investigator's wrong hypotheses are not shown — they are always right, which converts investigation to oracle.

## AI WRITING DEFAULTS TO AVOID
The writing system will produce an investigator who receives information rather than gathering it → Passive information receipt is not investigation → The investigator must actively work for each piece of information; it must cost something to obtain.

The writing system will produce investigative conclusions without investigative process → Conclusions without process converts the investigator to a plot device → Show the specific steps of reasoning and gathering that produce each conclusion.
