## FILE ID
supernatural-mechanic-operative

## DIMENSION TYPE
Supernatural Mechanic

## ONE-LINE DEFINITION
The supernatural exists, operates according to internal rules, and affects plot outcomes — but it is a feature of the world rather than the story's subject.

## FUNCTIONAL DEFINITION
Operative supernatural mechanic establishes the supernatural as a real, rule-governed feature of the story's world without making the investigation of the supernatural the story's central concern. The supernatural is infrastructure, not subject matter. A character can cast a spell or be haunted by a ghost and the story continues to be about something else — the relationship, the investigation, the political intrigue — while the supernatural participates in events.

The critical requirement is rule-governance. Operative supernatural that operates without consistent rules is not operative — it is authorial convenience. The reader must be able to understand what the supernatural can and cannot do, what it costs, what its limits are. These rules do not need to be fully explained, but they must be consistent. When the supernatural violates its own rules, the reader knows.

At the structural level, operative supernatural works like technology or institutional power — it is a resource with rules and costs that characters can use or be used by. A character who can communicate with the dead can receive information that way; they cannot receive information the dead person didn't have. A character who is haunted is haunted in specific ways; the haunting has specific effects and specific limits.

The failure mode is when operative supernatural becomes central without the story acknowledging the shift — the supernatural begins to dominate the story's attention at the expense of whatever the story was actually about.

## READER PSYCHOLOGY
Operative supernatural gives readers the experience of a world with additional rules — the pleasure of a fully realized alternate world where the supernatural is as reliable (and as unreliable) as any other system. This is the specific pleasure of urban fantasy and many paranormal genres: the reader can track the supernatural's logic and feel the satisfaction of consistent world-building.

Readers who respond to operative supernatural want the supernatural to be real and consistent in ways they can understand and predict. The register provides the pleasure of expertise — the reader learns the rules and can apply them.

What operative withholds is mystery. The supernatural in operative mechanic is not magical in the sense of being unknowable — it is a system with rules. Readers who want the supernatural to remain uncanny will find operative mechanic demystifying.

## PROSE SIGNATURE
- Supernatural events are described in the same matter-of-fact register as mundane events — they are part of the world's furniture, not spectacle.
- Rules of the supernatural are established through demonstration rather than exposition — the reader learns what the supernatural can do by watching it do things, not by being told.
- Costs and limits of supernatural capability are shown before or immediately after the capability is demonstrated — the supernatural is never free.
- Characters who use or encounter the supernatural behave as competent people behave with any tool or system — they work within its limits.
- The supernatural's effects on plot are causal and traceable — what the supernatural produced could not have been produced otherwise, and what it could not produce is equally clear.
- Wonder or dread at the supernatural is registered in proportion to what has happened — not inflated for spectacle, not deflated through irony.

## CALIBRATION EXAMPLES
**Neil Gaiman, American Gods** — The gods operate through consistent rules that emerge through demonstration. Shadow's experience of the supernatural is always grounded in the specific capabilities and limitations of specific gods. Gaiman never inflates the supernatural beyond what its established rules would support.

**Jim Butcher, Storm Front** — The magic system is explicitly rule-governed and Dresden's capabilities are consistently established and consistently tested. The supernatural is infrastructure: Dresden is a detective who also does magic; the magic is operative but the story is detective fiction.

**Nnedi Ofofor, Who Fears Death** — The supernatural power operates through specific physical and emotional costs that are tracked throughout the novel. Ofofor's prose never lets the supernatural exceed its established rules even when the plot would benefit from it doing so.

**NEAR-MISS — Deborah Harkness, A Discovery of Witches** — The supernatural is present and has rules, but the rules are established through exposition rather than demonstration, and the story's attention drifts toward the romance at the cost of the operative mechanics. The near-miss failure is that the supernatural becomes atmospheric rather than operative in the middle third of the novel — the rules are present but the supernatural stops producing plot-level consequences.

## FAILURE MODES
- The supernatural rules are inconsistent — capabilities that existed earlier are absent when the plot needs them absent, without explanation.
- The supernatural is used to solve problems the story could not solve through non-supernatural means without first establishing that the supernatural could do what it did — deus ex machina.
- The cost of the supernatural is established but not paid — characters use supernatural capabilities without the established consequences arriving.
- The supernatural migrates from operative to central without the story acknowledging the shift — it begins to dominate scenes that are not about it.
- Rules are established through exposition blocks rather than through demonstration — the reader is told what the supernatural can do rather than watching it do it.

## AI WRITING DEFAULTS TO AVOID
The writing system will use the supernatural to resolve plot problems that have not been set up as solvable through the established supernatural capabilities → Supernatural problem-solving without prior establishment is deus ex machina → The supernatural can only solve problems it has already been established as capable of solving; set up capabilities before deploying them.

The writing system will produce supernatural capabilities without costs or limits → Uncostly supernatural is convenient rather than operative → Every supernatural capability must have an established limit and an established cost; show the limit before the story needs it.

The writing system will describe supernatural events with elevated prose that signals spectacle → Elevation converts operative to central by making the supernatural the scene's focus → Supernatural events receive the same prose temperature as mundane events.
