## FILE ID
tone-register-procedural-cold

## DIMENSION TYPE
Tone Register

## ONE-LINE DEFINITION
Emotional affect is minimized — events are reported; their significance is left to the reader to supply.

## FUNCTIONAL DEFINITION
Procedural cold tone register minimizes the narrator's emotional investment in events to the point where the reader is responsible for supplying all emotional response. The prose reports. It does not react, mourn, celebrate, or editorialize. This is not the same as dry humor (which implies a perspective that finds things amusing) or grounded cynicism (which implies a perspective that has accepted things) — procedural cold has withdrawn perspective to the level of reportage.

The register is difficult to sustain because the reader's emotional engagement requires some tethering to the narrative. Pure procedural cold produces disconnection. The technique works when it is a register choice for a specific narrator or section, not when it is the default mode of a narrator who has simply failed to express feeling.

## PROSE SIGNATURE
- Passive construction appears more than in other registers — things happen to characters rather than characters experiencing things.
- Emotional states are reported as physical states — "she sat down" rather than "she was devastated."
- Scene endings provide closure through information rather than through feeling.
- Dialogue attribution is minimal — "he said" rather than "he said bitterly."
- Violence and loss are reported with the same prose temperature as administrative events.

## CALIBRATION EXAMPLES
**Cormac McCarthy, No Country for Old Men** — Chigurh's scenes are procedural cold at the sentence level. McCarthy's narration of violence is as unaffected as his narration of landscape.

**James M. Cain, The Postman Always Rings Twice** — The narrator reports the murder and its aftermath in the same register as everything else. The coldness is not the narrator's emotional protection — it is the voice.

**NEAR-MISS — Gillian Flynn, Dark Places** — Flynn attempts procedural cold in some sections but breaks the register with ironic commentary that implies a perspective the register should withhold. The near-miss failure is that irony requires a point of view that procedural cold has suppressed.

## FAILURE MODES
- The cold register breaks in moments of high drama, where the prose inflates to match the event.
- Irony appears, which implies a perspective that procedural cold has withheld.
- The register is used as default rather than as choice — the story is cold because the writer hasn't made the register choice rather than because the cold serves the story.

## AI WRITING DEFAULTS TO AVOID
The writing system will produce emotional inflation at dramatic moments → Inflation breaks the procedural cold register precisely when it most needs to hold → The most dramatic events in a procedural cold story should receive the same prose temperature as the most mundane.

The writing system will signal the significance of events through pacing → Extended scene time signals importance; procedural cold does not signal importance → Events receive space proportional to their information content, not their emotional weight.
