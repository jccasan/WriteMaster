## FILE ID
plot-engine-pursuit

## DIMENSION TYPE
Plot Engine

## ONE-LINE DEFINITION
Someone is hunting someone — the dynamic of hunter and hunted shapes all scene logic, and proximity is the primary measure of plot advancement.

## FUNCTIONAL DEFINITION
Pursuit plot engine organizes the story around a specific two-party dynamic: one character or force is actively seeking another with intent to capture, destroy, or expose them. Every scene advances either the hunter's proximity or the hunted's evasion. Scenes that do neither break the engine.

Pursuit is distinct from survival: survival can be against systemic or environmental threat. Pursuit requires a specific relationship between specific parties whose relative positions are tracked throughout the story.

The engine can flip: the hunted becomes the hunter. These flips are the pursuit engine's primary source of structural surprise.

## READER PSYCHOLOGY
Pursuit engine gives readers the experience of tracking a specific relational dynamic. The reader measures the gap between hunter and hunted at each scene. Each chapter reveals whether the gap narrowed or widened — a precise and trackable form of tension.

## PROSE SIGNATURE
- Proximity is tracked spatially and temporally — the reader always knows approximately how close the hunter is.
- Each chapter ends with the gap explicitly or implicitly updated.
- Hunter and hunted scenes alternate in a rhythm that keeps both perspectives active.
- The hunted's decision-making is specific to evasion — choices are made against the hunter's specific capabilities.

## CALIBRATION EXAMPLES
**John Buchan, The Thirty-Nine Steps** — The pursuit engine in pure form. Every chapter either updates Hannay's proximity to the hunters or his evasion capability. The spatial relationship is explicit.

**Thomas Harris, Red Dragon** — The pursuit tracks investigative proximity alongside physical proximity — Graham's asymmetric access to the killer's psychology is what closes the gap.

**NEAR-MISS — Dan Brown, The Da Vinci Code** — The pursuit engine is present (Langdon is being hunted) but the hunter's proximity is not consistently tracked — the reader cannot tell how close Fache is at any given moment, which removes the tension that proximity tracking produces. The near-miss failure is that the pursuit exists as a premise rather than as a structurally tracked dynamic.

## FAILURE MODES
- Hunter and hunted fail to respond to each other's specific moves — pursuit proceeds as parallel action rather than a responsive relationship.
- Proximity is not updated across scenes — the reader loses track of the relative positions.
- The hunt is resolved by luck rather than by either party's adaptive capability.

## AI WRITING DEFAULTS TO AVOID
The writing system will produce hunter and hunted scenes that do not respond to each other → Responsive relationship is the engine's requirement → Each hunter scene must respond to the hunted's prior move; each hunted scene must respond to the hunter's prior move.

The writing system will produce static proximity → Static proximity removes the engine's core tension → Every scene must explicitly or implicitly update where the parties are relative to each other.

The writing system will produce pursuit without updating hunter capability → A hunter who doesn't get better positioned is not a credible threat → The hunter must adapt to the hunted's evasion or the hunt is not genuinely responsive.

## CONFLICT WITH OTHER DIMENSIONS

- Conflicts with: pacing-slow-burn
- What creates the friction: Slow-burn withholds advancement; pursuit requires proximity to update each chapter.
- Default winner: plot-engine-pursuit — engine requires proximity movement.
- Override condition: pacing-slow-burn wins when the pursuit is defined by accumulation of evidence that the hunter is closing in, rather than active physical movement — a psychological or institutional pursuit can be slow-burn.

- Conflicts with: moral-register-nihilistic
- What creates the friction: Nihilistic denies that effort produces reliable outcomes; pursuit requires that the hunter's or hunted's efforts meaningfully change the gap.
- Default winner: plot-engine-pursuit — the engine's mechanics require responsive causation that nihilistic denies.
- Override condition: moral-register-nihilistic wins when the pursuit is revealed to have been inevitable — the hunter always would have found the hunted regardless of evasion.

## COMBINATION NOTES

- Combines with: pacing-propulsive
- Emergent effect: Physical danger and narrative speed are the same sensation — proximity and pace increase together, producing a reading experience where danger and momentum are inseparable.

- Combines with: protagonist-structure-insider-turned-outsider
- Emergent effect: The insider-turned-outsider is hunted by the institution they formerly served — the hunt is about what they know and what the institution cannot allow public, producing pursuit that is simultaneously physical and informational.

- Combines with: supernatural-mechanic-operative
- Emergent effect: The hunter or hunted has operative supernatural capability — the proximity calculation includes supernatural dimensions that make the pursuit unpredictable in specific, rule-governed ways rather than arbitrarily.
