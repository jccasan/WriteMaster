## FILE ID
tone-register-morally-earnest

## DIMENSION TYPE
Tone Register

## ONE-LINE DEFINITION
The story takes its ethical stakes seriously and treats the reader as capable of engaging with moral weight without irony's protection.

## FUNCTIONAL DEFINITION
Morally earnest tone register commits the narrative to the genuine importance of the ethical questions it raises. The narrator does not stand at ironic distance from the story's moral stakes — they are invested in whether the right thing is done, what the right thing costs, and whether the characters can bear the cost. The reader is invited into the same investment.

This is not the same as didactic fiction: morally earnest does not tell the reader what to conclude. It takes the moral questions seriously enough that the reader is trusted to engage with them seriously.

The register's risk is sentimentality — the tone can tip from earnest to mawkish if the emotional stakes are elevated above what the story has earned.

## PROSE SIGNATURE
- The moral stakes are established through behavior before they are tested — the reader knows what is at risk because they have seen it.
- Emotional responses to moral events are given scene time proportional to their weight.
- Characters' moral reasoning is taken seriously and reported without deflation.
- The narrator does not protect the reader from the weight of the story's ethical events through irony.

## CALIBRATION EXAMPLES
**Ursula K. Le Guin, The Left Hand of Darkness** — The moral questions (about gender, loyalty, and political responsibility) are taken with complete seriousness. Le Guin's prose does not stand at ironic distance from Genly's moral struggles.

**Harper Lee, To Kill a Mockingbird** — The earnestness is structural: the story refuses to protect itself through irony. Atticus's moral position is reported without deflation.

**NEAR-MISS — Nicholas Sparks, The Notebook** — The earnest tone tips into sentimentality because the emotional stakes are elevated above what the story has earned through specific behavioral demonstration. The near-miss failure is that earnestness without restraint becomes mawkish.

## FAILURE MODES
- The earnestness tips into sentimentality — emotional stakes are elevated beyond what the story has earned.
- A character articulates the moral theme explicitly, converting earnest engagement to didacticism.
- The moral stakes are stated rather than demonstrated, requiring the reader to accept the story's assessment without evidence.

## AI WRITING DEFAULTS TO AVOID
The writing system will have characters state moral conclusions → Stated moral conclusions convert earnest to didactic → Moral positions emerge through behavior and consequence, not through what characters say they believe.

The writing system will produce emotional inflation → Inflation tips earnest into sentimental → Emotional responses are given scene time proportional to what has been earned, not to what the story wants the reader to feel.
