## FILE ID
plot-engine-information

## DIMENSION TYPE
Plot Engine

## ONE-LINE DEFINITION
The story is driven by what is known, unknown, and by whom — information asymmetry is the primary mechanism of conflict, alliance, and plot advancement.

## FUNCTIONAL DEFINITION
Information plot engine organizes the story around the gap between what characters know and what they don't, and the consequences of that gap. Every major plot event is either a revelation (information transfer that changes the power balance), a concealment (information withheld to maintain advantage), or a calculation error (action taken on incomplete information). The story advances when information moves, and the reader's engagement is with tracking who knows what.

This is different from revelation plot engine: revelation builds toward a single recontextualizing truth. Information is an ongoing economy — multiple pieces of information are in play simultaneously, their values shifting as characters learn and conceal and act on partial knowledge.

At the structural level, information plot engine requires the writer to maintain a map of knowledge: who knows what at each point in the story. Every scene must be consistent with this map, and every plot development must derive from it. Characters who act on information they shouldn't have breaks the engine; characters who fail to act on information they have also breaks it (unless the failure is itself explained by the engine).

## READER PSYCHOLOGY
Information plot engine gives readers the experience of tracking a complex epistemic game. The reader often has access to more information than individual characters, which produces dramatic irony: the reader watches characters make decisions on incomplete information, knowing what the characters don't. This is the engine's specific tension — the reader wants characters to know what the reader knows, but the story's architecture withholds.

What information withholds is visceral stakes. The tension is intellectual and strategic, not physical. Readers who want physical danger as their primary source of engagement will find pure information engine somewhat cold.

## PROSE SIGNATURE
- Dialogue carries load — what characters say and don't say, what they ask and don't ask, is where information moves.
- Scene construction is organized around what each character knows going in and what they know coming out.
- Physical action is often information-delivery in disguise — a character's movement reveals what they knew about who would be where.
- The narrator withholds from the reader in proportion to what the POV character doesn't know — the reader's information is tied to the protagonist's.
- Scenes of information transfer are paced slowly — the moment of revelation receives full scene time regardless of how brief the information itself is.
- False information is rendered with the same prose weight as true information — the reader cannot tell the difference until the story does.

## CALIBRATION EXAMPLES
**John le Carré, Tinker Tailor Soldier Spy** — The information map is the story. Every scene is an exercise in tracking who knows what and acting on incomplete knowledge. Le Carré's prose requires the reader to maintain the same careful epistemic tracking the characters must.

**Donna Tartt, The Secret History** — The reader knows the murder happened from page one; what they track through the novel is the information asymmetry between Richard (who knows what happened and conceals it) and the other characters (who don't). The engine is entirely informational.

## FAILURE MODES
- Characters act on information they shouldn't have — the information map is violated.
- Information transfers too quickly — revelations arrive without the dramatic preparation that makes them land.
- The information asymmetry is resolved too early — the story has nowhere to go once everyone knows the same thing.

## AI WRITING DEFAULTS TO AVOID
The writing system will have characters share information freely and completely in dialogue → Complete information sharing collapses the engine → Characters share what advances their interests and conceal what doesn't; every conversation is a strategic act.

The writing system will produce information revelations as monologue → Monologue delivers information without the strategic calculation of who is telling whom what → Information moves through interaction; revelations are decisions made by the character who has the information.

## CONFLICT WITH OTHER DIMENSIONS

- Conflicts with: moral-register-nihilistic
- What creates the friction: Information engine requires that knowledge matters — that knowing changes the power balance; nihilistic denies that knowledge produces reliable outcomes.
- Default winner: plot-engine-information — the plot engine governs structure more completely; knowledge can matter within a nihilistic story even if it doesn't produce justice.
- Override condition: moral-register-nihilistic wins when the information is revealed to be irrelevant to outcome — the protagonist learns everything and it changes nothing.

## COMBINATION NOTES

- Combines with: moral-register-noir
- Emergent effect: Knowledge accumulates without producing justice — the information engine drives the story forward while the moral register ensures that learning the truth changes only personal understanding, not systemic outcome.

- Combines with: protagonist-structure-investigator
- Emergent effect: The investigator's methodology is organized around information acquisition — each procedural step is an information move, and the investigation's progress is measured in information transferred rather than in events produced.

- Combines with: moral-register-grey
- Emergent effect: Knowledge makes moral clarity harder rather than easier — each piece of information reveals a new legitimate interest, inverting the detective story's promise of resolution through knowledge.
