## FILE ID
supernatural-mechanic-revelatory

## DIMENSION TYPE
Supernatural Mechanic

## ONE-LINE DEFINITION
The supernatural reveals something about the world or a character that could not be revealed through any non-supernatural means — it functions as epistemological event, not as capability or atmosphere.

## FUNCTIONAL DEFINITION
Revelatory supernatural mechanic uses the supernatural as a vehicle for a specific kind of knowledge: knowledge that could only arrive from outside ordinary human perception or causation. A ghost reveals what no living person knew. A vision shows what cannot be witnessed. A possession reveals an interior life that the host could never have articulated. The supernatural event is the story's instrument of revelation, not its subject.

This distinguishes revelatory from operative: operative supernatural affects plot outcomes through capability (the character can do something supernatural); revelatory supernatural produces plot outcomes through knowledge (the character learns something supernatural). And revelatory differs from central: central makes the supernatural's nature and rules the story's investigation; revelatory uses the supernatural to investigate something else — a character's truth, a historical event, a moral reality.

At the structural level, revelatory supernatural is organized around what cannot be known through ordinary means and what knowing it costs. The revelation typically arrives at a high cost to the character who receives it, or arrives too late to produce the outcome it would otherwise have enabled. The supernatural's knowledge is real but its utility is complicated.

The technique fails when the revelation is available through non-supernatural means — when a character's ghost reveals something a living witness could have revealed, the supernatural was not necessary and its presence reads as decorative.

## READER PSYCHOLOGY
Revelatory supernatural gives readers the experience of truth arriving through extraordinary means — the specific satisfaction of a knowledge that exceeded what was available and was delivered by forces outside normal causation. This produces a specific emotional register: not the awe of the uncanny (atmospheric) or the competence of the rule-governed system (operative), but the grief or wonder of knowing something you couldn't have known.

Readers who respond to revelatory supernatural are drawn to the convergence of supernatural event and emotional truth — the moment when what a ghost reveals is also what the reader needed to understand about the protagonist's relationship with what they lost.

What revelatory withholds is operational power. The knowledge arrives but cannot always be acted on. The supernatural reveals what was true; it does not give the character the ability to change it.

## PROSE SIGNATURE
- The supernatural revelation arrives through a specific event that is localized and singular — it does not recur in the same form.
- What is revealed is specific and personal rather than cosmic or abstract — the revelation concerns a particular truth about a particular situation.
- The cost of receiving the revelation is established before or immediately after it arrives — the knowledge is not free.
- The protagonist's response to the revelation is behavioral rather than cognitive — they do not process and understand, they act or fail to act.
- The revelation is presented as information, not as spectacle — the supernatural event itself is secondary to what it disclosed.
- What the revelation cannot tell the protagonist is as important as what it does tell them — the limits of supernatural knowledge matter.

## CALIBRATION EXAMPLES
**Toni Morrison, Beloved** — Beloved's presence reveals what Sethe cannot remember and what the community needs to understand — a specific historical trauma made present through supernatural means that could not have been present otherwise. Morrison's prose renders Beloved's revelations as embodied knowledge rather than as information.

**Kazuo Ishiguro, The Buried Giant** — The memory-erasing mist and the dragon Querig function as revelatory mechanics: when their influence is removed, the revelations that emerge are specifically truths that could not have been accessed through ordinary memory or ordinary history.

**Gabriel García Márquez, One Hundred Years of Solitude** — The supernatural elements consistently function as revelatory — the protagonist's clairvoyance, the insomnia plague, the repeated events across generations all reveal truths about the Buendía family that ordinary narrative access could not have delivered.

**NEAR-MISS — Alice Sebold, The Lovely Bones** — The murdered narrator's supernatural vantage point reveals information about the investigation and her family, but the revelations could largely have been delivered through other narrative means (a living investigator, her family members' perspectives). The near-miss failure is that the supernatural mechanism is not genuinely epistemologically necessary — the knowledge it delivers could have been obtained otherwise, making the supernatural decorative rather than genuinely revelatory.

## FAILURE MODES
- The revelation delivers information that could have been obtained through non-supernatural means — the supernatural was not epistemologically necessary.
- The revelation arrives without cost — the protagonist receives important knowledge without anything being required of them.
- The revelation is abstract rather than specific — a general truth rather than a particular fact about a particular situation.
- The protagonist responds to the revelation with cognitive processing rather than behavioral change or paralysis — revelation should move people, not inform them.
- The same revelatory mechanism is repeated — the second revelation through the same supernatural vehicle diminishes the first.

## AI WRITING DEFAULTS TO AVOID
The writing system will produce supernatural revelations that confirm what the protagonist already suspected → Confirmation is not revelation; the supernatural should deliver knowledge that was genuinely unavailable → The revelation must show the protagonist something that contradicts, recontextualizes, or exceeds their existing understanding.

The writing system will produce revelatory events through exposition — the ghost explains what happened → Explanation is cognitive delivery; revelation should arrive through image, sensation, or event that the protagonist must interpret → The supernatural revelation shows rather than tells; the protagonist must do the interpretive work.

The writing system will produce revelation without cost → Costless revelation is convenient rather than supernatural → What the protagonist receives through supernatural knowledge must require something of them — in grief, in obligation, in action.

## CONFLICT WITH OTHER DIMENSIONS

- Conflicts with: supernatural-mechanic-atmospheric
- What creates the friction: Atmospheric sustains uncertainty about whether the supernatural is real; revelatory requires the supernatural to have delivered specific, reliable information.
- Default winner: supernatural-mechanic-revelatory — the revelation commits the story to the supernatural's reality in a way atmospheric cannot recover from.
- Override condition: supernatural-mechanic-atmospheric wins in a story where the revelatory event might be interpreted as hallucination or wish-fulfillment — where the revelation's reliability is itself uncertain.

- Conflicts with: supernatural-mechanic-absent
- What creates the friction: Absent commits to human-only causation; revelatory requires the supernatural to have delivered knowledge.
- Default winner: supernatural-mechanic-revelatory — mutually exclusive once the revelation occurs.
- Override condition: None — the revelation is either real or it is not.

## COMBINATION NOTES

- Combines with: moral-register-tragic
- Emergent effect: The supernatural reveals what the protagonist could have known in time to prevent the tragedy — the revelatory mechanic delivers the truth too late, or delivers it to someone who cannot act on it, making the tragedy specifically about the impossibility of the right knowledge arriving at the right time.

- Combines with: plot-engine-revelation
- Emergent effect: The plot's revelatory arc and the supernatural mechanic are aligned — the truth that recontextualizes everything arrives through supernatural means, which means the revelation is both structurally necessary and arrived at through forces outside ordinary causation.

- Combines with: protagonist-structure-insider-turned-outsider
- Emergent effect: The supernatural reveals what the insider knew before they were excluded — giving the protagonist back a specific piece of their former access through supernatural rather than institutional means, producing a specific kind of partial restoration.

- Combines with: moral-register-grey
- Emergent effect: The supernatural revelation does not simplify the moral question — it reveals that the forces behind the events had legitimate competing claims, making the supernatural itself a participant in the grey moral architecture rather than an external arbiter.
