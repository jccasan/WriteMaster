## FILE ID
protagonist-structure-operative

## DIMENSION TYPE
Protagonist Structure

## ONE-LINE DEFINITION
A trained professional whose competence is the primary site of characterization — the story tests their skills and then tests their values.

## FUNCTIONAL DEFINITION
Operative protagonist structure makes professional competence the character's primary expression. Who they are is visible in how they work: their methodology, their judgment under pressure, their professional relationships, and the specific texture of their expertise. The operative is defined before the story begins by their training and experience — the story does not need to establish their competence; it can immediately test it.

The structure has two sequential phases: first, the story tests the operative's skills (are they as good as their reputation?); then, it tests their values (what will they refuse to do, and at what cost?). The second phase is where character depth lives. An operative whose skills are tested but whose values are never threatened is a competence fantasy, not a character study.

## READER PSYCHOLOGY
Operative protagonist gives readers the specific pleasure of watching expertise in action. The reader is invited to admire competence and then to care about the person whose competence they admire. What this structure withholds is interiority as a primary value — the operative's inner life is expressed through their professional choices, not through reflection.

## PROSE SIGNATURE
- Competence is demonstrated through what the operative does, not through other characters' testimony about them.
- Interior processing happens in motion — if the operative thinks, they think while doing something professionally relevant.
- Values appear through refusals — what the operative will not do is as important as what they will.
- Professional relationships carry emotional weight — trust and betrayal among operatives register more intensely than equivalent personal betrayals.

## CALIBRATION EXAMPLES
**John le Carré, The Spy Who Came in from the Cold** — Leamas's competence is established through behavior before it is tested by the plot. The value test — what he discovers he was used to do — is the novel's actual subject.

**Michael Mann, Heat (screenplay)** — Both Neil McCauley and Vincent Hanna are operatives; the story is organized around their professional competence first and their personal costs second.

**NEAR-MISS — Vince Flynn, Mitch Rapp series** — The operative structure is present but the value test is absent — Rapp's values are never seriously threatened or required to cost him anything. The near-miss failure is that the story tests competence without testing values, producing competence fantasy rather than character study.

## FAILURE MODES
- Competence is asserted through other characters' admiration rather than demonstrated through the operative's actions.
- The value test never arrives — the operative's ethics are never seriously challenged.
- The operative's inner life is given extended reflective scenes that aren't tied to professional decision-making.

## AI WRITING DEFAULTS TO AVOID
The writing system will produce an operative who is competent without cost → Costless competence is fantasy → Demonstrate competence through situations that require genuine judgment and produce genuine mistakes.

The writing system will produce an operative whose values are explicitly stated in dialogue or interior monologue → Stated values replace demonstrated values → Values appear through what the operative refuses to do, not through what they say they believe.
