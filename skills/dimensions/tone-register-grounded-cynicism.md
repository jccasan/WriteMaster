## FILE ID
tone-register-grounded-cynicism

## DIMENSION TYPE
Tone Register

## ONE-LINE DEFINITION
The narrator sees clearly and accepts what they see — cynicism as survival strategy and as the only honest response to experience, without despair.

## FUNCTIONAL DEFINITION
Grounded cynicism tone register positions the narrator (or the close third limited narrator's voice) in a specific relationship to the story's world: they see it accurately, they accept it without surprise, and they continue to act inside it without either the consolation of optimism or the paralysis of despair. This is the distinction from nihilism: nihilism denies that action matters; grounded cynicism acts anyway while harboring no illusions.

The register produces a specific prose voice: dry, precise, without elevation, occasionally darkly funny. The narrator reports on human failure with the matter-of-fact tone of someone who expected human failure and is neither surprised nor destroyed by it.

## PROSE SIGNATURE
- Irony is structural rather than verbal — the gap between what things claim to be and what they are is visible in the prose without being commented on.
- Dark humor arrives from juxtaposition — what is funny is the gap between official language and actual event.
- Physical description is precise and unromantic — things are described as they are, without aestheticizing.
- Emotional responses are brief and specific — the cynic does not dwell; they register and continue.

## CALIBRATION EXAMPLES
**Raymond Chandler, The Big Sleep** — Marlowe's voice is the canonical grounded cynicism register: he sees the corruption, he expects it, he continues working. "I had the air-conditioned feeling of a man who had wasted his time."

**Elmore Leonard, Get Shorty** — Leonard's narration is grounded cynicism in its purest form: the narrator watches human venality with something like affection, because it is at least consistent.

**NEAR-MISS — Bret Easton Ellis, American Psycho** — The narration attempts grounded cynicism but tips into nihilism: the register cannot sustain equanimity because Patrick Bateman's worldview produces self-destruction. The near-miss failure is that the narrator cannot maintain the grounded part — the cynicism becomes a symptom rather than a survival strategy.

## FAILURE MODES
- The cynicism tips into despair — the narrator stops acting or caring, converting grounded cynicism to nihilism.
- Dark humor is explained or signaled — the register requires the humor to be visible without the narrator commenting that it is funny.
- The cynical narrator is secretly sentimental — the cynicism is a pose that the story removes to reveal a warmer interior.

## AI WRITING DEFAULTS TO AVOID
The writing system will produce a cynical narrator who is secretly idealistic → Secret idealism converts cynicism to pose → The cynical narrator genuinely believes what they say about the world while continuing to act inside it.

The writing system will signal dark humor → Signaled humor kills it → The gap between expectation and reality should produce the humor; the narrator does not comment on it.
