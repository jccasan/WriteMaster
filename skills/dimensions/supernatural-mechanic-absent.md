## FILE ID
supernatural-mechanic-absent

## DIMENSION TYPE
Supernatural Mechanic

## ONE-LINE DEFINITION
No supernatural elements exist in the story's world — any strangeness is psychological, institutional, or the result of human behavior taken to its extreme.

## FUNCTIONAL DEFINITION
Absent supernatural mechanic is not a neutral default — it is a positive commitment. By choosing absent, the story makes a specific claim: everything that happens in this world has a human, institutional, or natural explanation. This claim shapes what is available to the narrative. Strange events must be explained through the logic of how people behave under pressure, how institutions produce irrational outcomes through the accumulation of rational individual decisions, or how coincidence and human limitation produce patterns that appear meaningful but aren't.

The absent mechanic often works through misdirection — allowing the reader to wonder momentarily whether something supernatural is occurring, then providing a grounded explanation. This structure is different from atmospheric supernatural, which allows the reader to remain uncertain. Absent commits: whatever seemed strange has a human cause.

At the prose level, absent supernatural requires the world to be fully material and causally coherent. If something cannot be explained, the story must either provide the explanation or make clear that an explanation exists and will eventually be found. The sensation of the uncanny, when the story produces it, is always ultimately located in human behavior rather than in any force outside human agency.

This is not a limitation. Absent supernatural is the right choice for stories where the horror, the wonder, or the strangeness are specifically located in what human beings are capable of. The most disturbing things in an absent-supernatural story are people.

## READER PSYCHOLOGY
Absent supernatural mechanic gives readers the experience of a story where the full weight of what happens falls on human agency. Nothing is diluted by mystery or wonder — if something terrible happened, a person chose it or created the conditions for it. This is specifically what atmospheric and operative supernatural do not provide: in absent mechanic stories, there is no force to blame that isn't human.

Readers who respond to absent supernatural are often readers who find the human capacity for harm or institutional failure more frightening than any supernatural force. The register validates the intuition that the most disturbing things are the things that can be explained.

What absent withholds is the relief of inexplicability. Adjacent mechanics allow the reader to locate strangeness outside human agency. Absent refuses this — the full weight of everything remains on people and institutions.

## PROSE SIGNATURE
- Strange or disturbing events are followed by the procedure of explanation — the narrative does not leave the reader with unresolved inexplicability.
- Sensory details that might suggest the uncanny are grounded in material cause — what sounds like something supernatural is traced to its mechanical or human source.
- Character perceptions of the strange are themselves subject to explanation — the protagonist who thinks they are experiencing something supernatural is either wrong or in a heightened psychological state that is itself explained.
- Institutional behavior is rendered through its irrational outcomes without assigning mystical cause — bureaucracies produce strange effects through ordinary process.
- The logic of cause and effect is maintained throughout — everything that happens has a prior cause that could, in principle, be traced.
- Wonder, when present, is located in human capability or natural phenomenon — the story's capacity for awe is fully material.

## CALIBRATION EXAMPLES
**Thomas Harris, The Silence of the Lambs** — The horror is entirely human. Hannibal Lecter's intelligence and Buffalo Bill's pathology are rendered without any suggestion of supernatural origin. Harris's prose commits completely to absent mechanic: every disturbing element has a psychological or behavioral explanation that the story eventually provides.

**John le Carré, The Spy Who Came in from the Cold** — The institutional machinations that produce the novel's ending are stranger than anything supernatural could be, and le Carré commits completely to the human and institutional explanation. The horror is in how the system worked, not in anything outside the system.

**Donna Tartt, The Secret History** — The characters commit ritualistic acts that gesture toward the supernatural, but Tartt's architecture is committed to absent mechanic: everything, including the ritual killing, has psychological and social explanations. The ritual produces a real death through entirely human means.

**NEAR-MISS — Peter Straub, Ghost Story** — The novel presents itself as horror with natural explanations possible, then commits to the supernatural explanation. The near-miss failure is that the absent-mechanic reader experience (sustained human-origin dread) is built over the first half of the novel and then abandoned when the supernatural explanation is confirmed — the architecture built one mechanic and then delivered another.

## FAILURE MODES
- The story leaves an event unexplained when the absent mechanic requires explanation to exist and be eventually provided.
- Character perception of the supernatural is used for atmosphere without the narrative grounding it — the reader experiences the uncanny without the committed payoff that absent requires.
- Institutional or psychological strangeness is romanticized to the point where it exceeds plausible human causation.
- The story implies that a natural explanation exists but never provides it, leaving the reader with a structurally open question the absent mechanic requires to be closed.

## AI WRITING DEFAULTS TO AVOID
The writing system will produce atmosphere that suggests the supernatural without committing to its absence → Unresolved supernatural suggestion violates the absent mechanic's commitment → If an event or perception seems supernatural, the narrative must either provide the grounded explanation or set up the eventual provision of one.

The writing system will produce characters whose capabilities are so extreme they strain the absent mechanic → Superhuman competence or pathology without grounding reads as latent supernatural → Keep human capability within ranges that remain explicable through psychology, training, or circumstance.

The writing system will leave institutional strangeness unexamined → In absent mechanic, institutional strangeness must be traceable to its human causes → Show the ordinary decisions that accumulated into the strange outcome; the bureaucratic logic must be visible.

## CONFLICT WITH OTHER DIMENSIONS

- Conflicts with: supernatural-mechanic-operative
- What creates the friction: Operative requires the supernatural to exist and affect outcomes; absent requires everything to have a human or natural explanation.
- Default winner: supernatural-mechanic-absent — these are mutually exclusive; a story cannot commit to absent and introduce operative supernatural.
- Override condition: None — these mechanics are architecturally incompatible. A story can transition from apparent absent to operative (the reader discovers the supernatural is real), but that transition is a structural choice, not a coexistence.

- Conflicts with: supernatural-mechanic-central
- What creates the friction: Central makes the supernatural the story's subject; absent denies the supernatural's existence.
- Default winner: supernatural-mechanic-absent — mutually exclusive architectures.
- Override condition: None.

- Conflicts with: supernatural-mechanic-atmospheric
- What creates the friction: Atmospheric sustains supernatural uncertainty; absent commits to grounded explanation.
- Default winner: supernatural-mechanic-absent — absent requires commitment that atmospheric refuses to make.
- Override condition: supernatural-mechanic-atmospheric can operate during a sequence where the absent-mechanic story has not yet provided the explanation — the atmosphere of uncertainty is valid until the explanation arrives.

## COMBINATION NOTES

- Combines with: plot-engine-revelation
- Emergent effect: The revelation that resolves the story is entirely human in origin — the truth that recontextualizes everything is a truth about what people did, which is more disturbing than any supernatural revelation because it cannot be located outside human agency.

- Combines with: moral-register-grey
- Emergent effect: With no supernatural to assign agency to, the grey moral weight falls entirely on competing human positions — the genuinely impossible moral choice is between people with legitimate claims, not between human and supernatural force.

- Combines with: protagonist-structure-investigator
- Emergent effect: The investigation is committed to finding a human explanation and finding it — the investigator's rationalism is not tested by supernatural events but by the human capacity for behavior that exceeds easy explanation, producing a story about the limits of human comprehension rather than the limits of rational inquiry.
