## FILE ID
tone-register-mounting-dread

## DIMENSION TYPE
Tone Register

## ONE-LINE DEFINITION
Something terrible is coming — the story is organized around its approach, and the reader inhabits the space between certainty and arrival.

## FUNCTIONAL DEFINITION
Mounting dread tone register makes the approach of something terrible the reader's primary experience. The reader knows something bad is coming — or suspects it, or fears it might be coming — and the story is organized around the lengthening of that approach. Dread is not suspense: suspense is uncertainty about what will happen; dread is the certainty or near-certainty that something bad will happen and the agonizing space before it does.

The register requires that the thing coming be specific enough that the reader can fear it but undefined enough that imagination can expand it. Overdefined dread collapses into suspense. Underdefined dread becomes vague anxiety without direction.

## PROSE SIGNATURE
- Details that might be neutral in another register read as ominous here — the prose temperature prepares the reader to interpret innocuous things as threatening.
- The protagonist's ordinary activities are described against the reader's foreknowledge of what is approaching.
- Sensory detail is heightened in scenes of dread — what the character notices is specific and slightly wrong.
- Pacing slows before the thing arrives — the prose gives the reader more time in the space before.

## CALIBRATION EXAMPLES
**Shirley Jackson, We Have Always Lived in the Castle** — The dread is for the outside world penetrating the castle's safety. Jackson's prose makes every approaching event (the cousin's visit, the fire) feel like a step toward catastrophe.

**du Maurier, Jamaica Inn** — The approach of revelation and violence is built through specific environmental detail that is always slightly threatening before the threat materializes.

**NEAR-MISS — Daphne du Maurier, Rebecca** — Rebecca attempts mounting dread but the dread is frequently replaced by anxiety about social status. The near-miss failure is that the tonal register is interrupted by concerns that are embarrassment rather than existential dread, breaking the sustained approach.

## FAILURE MODES
- The thing coming arrives too quickly — dread requires extended approach.
- The thing coming is too well-defined — the reader's imagination cannot expand it.
- The narrative releases dread before the thing arrives — a scene of comfort or normality without the register's dark undertone provides relief that dread cannot recover from.

## AI WRITING DEFAULTS TO AVOID
The writing system will produce dread as explicit statement → "She felt that something terrible was coming" → Dread is not felt by the narrator; it is felt by the reader through what the prose withholds and what it over-specifies → Produce the reader's dread through sensory specificity and structural withholding, not through narrative statement.

The writing system will resolve the dread before the thing arrives → Pre-arrival resolution is false climax → The dread must sustain until the arrival; partial relief followed by reinstated dread can work but must be carefully controlled.
