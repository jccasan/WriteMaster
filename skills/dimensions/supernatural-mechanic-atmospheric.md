## FILE ID
supernatural-mechanic-atmospheric

## DIMENSION TYPE
Supernatural Mechanic

## ONE-LINE DEFINITION
The supernatural is suggested but never confirmed — the uncanny is present as mood and perception, but its existence is never established as causal fact.

## FUNCTIONAL DEFINITION
Atmospheric supernatural mechanic uses the tools of the supernatural — the uncanny, the inexplicable, the sensory experience of something that exceeds normal cause and effect — without committing to the supernatural as a real feature of the story's world. Events that might be supernatural are presented from a perspective that cannot definitively rule them out. The story refuses to confirm or deny.

This is different from absent mechanic: absent commits to grounded explanation. Atmospheric commits to sustaining uncertainty. The reader cannot know whether the supernatural is real in this story's world, and the story does not intend for them to find out.

At the craft level, this requires rigorous control of narrative perspective. The atmospheric supernatural operates through subjective experience — what characters perceive, what they cannot explain, what they fear might be true — without the narrator ever stepping outside that subjectivity to confirm or deny. The uncanny is always located in a character's perception rather than in objective narrative fact.

The risk is that atmospheric supernatural can slide into evasion — refusing to commit because the writer hasn't decided what is real. The technique requires commitment to the sustained uncertainty as an end in itself, not as a placeholder for a decision that hasn't been made.

## READER PSYCHOLOGY
Atmospheric supernatural gives readers the experience of prolonged dread — the specific fear that comes from not knowing whether the rules of the world are reliable. This is what absent and operative mechanics both refuse: absent resolves, operative establishes. Atmospheric sustains the uncertainty indefinitely, which produces a distinct reader experience — the reader inhabits the protagonist's epistemological instability.

Readers who respond to atmospheric supernatural are drawn to the specific experience of not knowing whether to be afraid of the thing that might not be there. The uncertainty is the experience, not the anticipation of resolution.

What atmospheric withholds is confirmation. The reader who wants to know whether the ghost is real will never be answered. Readers who require resolution will experience atmospheric as frustrating or evasive.

## PROSE SIGNATURE
- Uncanny events are described through a character's perception without narrative validation — what she saw, what seemed to happen, what could not be explained.
- Sensory detail is hyper-specific in uncanny sequences — the texture of the supernatural suggestion is built through precise rather than vague observation, because precision is what makes uncertainty credible.
- Rational explanations are made available but slightly insufficient — the character can talk themselves out of believing, but the explanation doesn't quite close the question.
- The narrator does not access what is happening outside the character's perception during uncanny sequences — no omniscient confirmation or denial.
- Repetition of specific details across multiple uncanny sequences builds a pattern that suggests meaning without establishing it.
- The prose temperature of uncanny sequences matches ordinary sequences — neither elevated nor minimized, which prevents both aestheticization and irony.

## CALIBRATION EXAMPLES
**Shirley Jackson, The Haunting of Hill House** — The famous opening paragraph establishes the atmospheric mechanic precisely: Hill House is described in terms that suggest sentience, but always through the narrator's attribution rather than narrative fact. Jackson never confirms whether Hill House is supernatural. Eleanor's final drive is rendered in a way that can be read as supernatural possession or as dissociative suicidal episode — the mechanic is sustained to the last sentence.

**Henry James, The Turn of the Screw** — The governess's account is the only source of the supernatural claim, and James structures the narrative so that the reliability of that account is permanently in question. The ghosts may be real or they may be the governess's pathology. The text does not decide and cannot be made to decide.

**Sarah Waters, The Little Stranger** — The supernatural incidents can be explained by the protagonist's repressed psychology or by the house's genuine haunting. Waters's prose sustains both readings simultaneously, which is the atmospheric mechanic's most demanding form.

**NEAR-MISS — Daphne du Maurier, Rebecca** — The second Mrs. de Winter's experience of Rebecca's presence is clearly rendered as psychological rather than supernatural, making Rebecca atmospheric in its first reading but absent-mechanic in its architecture. The near-miss failure is that du Maurier's narrative perspective eventually grants the reader access to information that makes the psychological explanation authoritative, which resolves the uncertainty the atmospheric mechanic requires.

## FAILURE MODES
- The narrative grants the reader access to information that definitively resolves whether the supernatural is real — any information outside the character's subjective experience breaks the mechanic.
- Uncanny events are described in elevated or horror-coded prose, which signals the supernatural's reality through tonal commitment.
- The rational explanation offered for uncanny events is so obviously insufficient that the supernatural is implicitly confirmed.
- A character explicitly endorses the supernatural explanation in a way the narrative does not undercut — granting the character's supernatural belief authority collapses the uncertainty.
- The story ends in a way that definitively resolves the supernatural question through plot outcome.

## AI WRITING DEFAULTS TO AVOID
The writing system will confirm supernatural events by placing them in third-person narration that exceeds character perspective → Third-person confirmation is not atmospheric — it is operative → Supernatural events must remain inside character perception; the narrator cannot see what the character cannot.

The writing system will produce uncanny sequences in elevated, horror-coded diction → Elevated diction signals that the supernatural is real → Maintain the same prose temperature for uncanny sequences as for ordinary scenes; precision not elevation.

The writing system will resolve atmospheric uncertainty through character dialogue — a character who definitively believes or definitively disbelieves is validated → Character certainty collapses uncertainty → Both believing and disbelieving characters must be rendered with equal authority and equal limitation.

## CONFLICT WITH OTHER DIMENSIONS

- Conflicts with: supernatural-mechanic-absent
- What creates the friction: Absent commits to grounded explanation; atmospheric commits to sustained uncertainty.
- Default winner: supernatural-mechanic-absent — if the story has committed to absent, uncanny sequences must resolve into human explanation; they cannot sustain the uncertainty atmospheric requires.
- Override condition: supernatural-mechanic-atmospheric wins during sequences before the grounded explanation has been provided — the atmosphere of uncertainty is valid until absent commits.

- Conflicts with: supernatural-mechanic-operative
- What creates the friction: Operative establishes the supernatural as causally real; atmospheric refuses to confirm it.
- Default winner: supernatural-mechanic-operative — once operative supernatural is established, atmospheric uncertainty is structurally impossible; the reader knows the supernatural is real.
- Override condition: supernatural-mechanic-atmospheric can survive operative mechanics in a different register — the reader knows the supernatural operates but specific instances remain uncertain.

- Conflicts with: supernatural-mechanic-central
- What creates the friction: Central makes the supernatural's nature and rules the story's subject; atmospheric refuses to establish the supernatural's nature.
- Default winner: supernatural-mechanic-central — investigation of the supernatural's nature is incompatible with sustained uncertainty about its existence.
- Override condition: None — central investigation cannot coexist with atmospheric uncertainty about whether the supernatural exists.

## COMBINATION NOTES

- Combines with: pacing-slow-burn
- Emergent effect: Supernatural suggestion accumulates across the story's length without ever resolving into confirmation — the slow-burn withholds events while the atmospheric mechanic withholds confirmation, producing a story where both pacing and mechanic are organized around the same sustained deferral.

- Combines with: moral-register-noir
- Emergent effect: The supernatural suggestion reads as the protagonist's perception of a corrupt world manifesting in uncanny form — the atmospheric mechanic becomes the subjective experience of the noir structure, where the corruption is so thorough it seems to exceed human cause.

- Combines with: tone-register-mounting-dread
- Emergent effect: Dread for something that may not exist — the mounting fear is specifically for an undefined threat, and the atmospheric mechanic ensures that the threat's nature remains undefined even as the reader's fear of it increases.

- Combines with: protagonist-structure-investigator
- Emergent effect: The investigator investigates but the investigation cannot resolve the supernatural question — their methodology produces evidence but not answers, which is the specific frustration of the atmospheric mechanic applied to the investigative structure.
