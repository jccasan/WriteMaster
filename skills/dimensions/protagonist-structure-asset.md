## FILE ID
protagonist-structure-asset

## DIMENSION TYPE
Protagonist Structure

## ONE-LINE DEFINITION
Someone being used by larger forces — with or without their knowledge — and the story is about the discovery of that use and the attempt to recover genuine agency.

## FUNCTIONAL DEFINITION
Asset protagonist structure places the protagonist in a position where their actions serve interests they do not fully understand or endorse. They may know they are being used (the witting asset) or may not know until the revelation (the unwitting asset). Either way, the story is organized around the gap between what the protagonist believes they are doing and what they are actually being made to do.

The structure's central question is agency: does the protagonist have genuine choices, or are their choices being managed? The story's movement is toward the protagonist discovering the full extent of their use and making a genuine choice about what to do with that knowledge.

## PROSE SIGNATURE
- The protagonist's ostensible goals are established with enough detail that the reader invests in them before discovering they are not the real goals.
- Information withheld from the protagonist is visible to the reader through what others say when the protagonist is not present.
- The asset's competence is real — they are not being used because they are weak, but because their specific capabilities serve the using party's needs.

## CALIBRATION EXAMPLES
**John le Carré, The Spy Who Came in from the Cold** — Leamas is a witting asset who discovers the extent of his use exceeds what he was told. Le Carré shows the using apparatus before revealing it to Leamas.

**Thomas Harris, The Silence of the Lambs** — Clarice is an asset of Crawford's in specific ways she only partially understands — used to access Lecter, used to investigate the case within Crawford's institutional constraints.

**NEAR-MISS — Dan Brown, The Da Vinci Code** — Langdon is technically an asset (he is being used by Sophie, by the Priory, by Teabing) but his asset status is not the story's subject — he is a protagonist who happens to be manipulated, not a protagonist whose relationship to manipulation is what the story investigates.

## FAILURE MODES
- The asset discovers their use and immediately recovers full agency without cost.
- The using party is simply villainous — the use lacks the institutional legitimacy that makes asset structure complex.
- The asset's capabilities are not specifically suited to the using party's needs — they are used arbitrarily.

## AI WRITING DEFAULTS TO AVOID
The writing system will reveal the asset's use through a villain's monologue → Monologue converts the revelation to information delivery → The asset discovers their use through what they observe, not through what they are told.

The writing system will produce an asset who recovers full agency immediately on discovering their use → Immediate recovery converts asset to operative → Agency recovery is difficult, partial, and costly.

## COMBINATION NOTES

- Combines with: plot-engine-revelation
- Emergent effect: The revelation that completes the revelation engine is the asset's discovery that they have been used — what they believed was their mission was constructed, and the revelation recontextualizes every prior action.

- Combines with: moral-register-nihilistic
- Emergent effect: The asset discovers that the forces using them have no genuine moral investment in the outcome — the asset's moral reasoning about their situation is correct but irrelevant.
