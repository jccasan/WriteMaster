## FILE ID
pacing-propulsive

## DIMENSION TYPE
Pacing

## ONE-LINE DEFINITION
Forward momentum is the primary narrative value — scene logic is subordinate to event logic, and every element of every chapter exists to advance to what happens next.

## FUNCTIONAL DEFINITION
Propulsive pacing treats narrative momentum as the story's first obligation. Scenes are shaped by what they must deliver to keep the story moving forward — what information, what decision, what event — and everything else in the scene is in service of that delivery. World-building that doesn't advance the plot is cut. Character interiority that doesn't produce a decision is compressed. Dialogue that doesn't deliver information or conflict is shortened.

This is not the same as thriller escalation, though they are related. Escalation requires that each chapter end at higher tension than it began — it is about the direction of the story's movement. Propulsive pacing is about the speed and momentum of the movement regardless of direction. A propulsive story can have chapters that land at lower tension than they began, as long as those chapters maintained momentum.

At the structural level, propulsive pacing organizes chapters around the question "what happens next?" rather than "what does this mean?" The chapter exists to answer the first question in a way that immediately raises it again. This is not a simple or easy technique — creating genuine propulsive momentum while maintaining character depth and thematic coherence requires that the events themselves carry the character and theme rather than those elements requiring separate scenes.

The critical craft challenge of propulsive pacing is that it can produce stories that feel eventful but thin — where so much happens that nothing registers as meaningful. The technique works when events are consequential as well as sequential.

## READER PSYCHOLOGY
Propulsive pacing produces the reading experience readers describe as compulsive — the story moves fast enough that putting it down requires a deliberate act of will. The reader is not contemplating what they have read; they are in motion toward what they will read. This is the register's specific offer and its specific limitation.

Readers who respond to propulsive pacing are readers who read primarily for story — for the experience of inhabiting a sequential narrative that keeps moving. The register does not offer depth of world, accumulation of texture, or the patience of long-form exploration. It offers forward motion as a value in itself.

What propulsive pacing withholds is dwell time. The reader cannot inhabit the story's world because the story keeps moving. Readers who want to live in a story rather than consume it will experience propulsive pacing as superficial.

## PROSE SIGNATURE
- Chapters open late — as close to the relevant event as possible, cutting all preceding material.
- Scene transitions prioritize the destination over the departure — "Three hours later, she was at the station" rather than "She packed her bag, hailed a cab, and arrived."
- Dialogue cuts as soon as information or conflict has been delivered — no trailing conversation after the scene's essential exchange.
- Interiority is brief and decision-adjacent — characters think when making a choice, not when processing what happened.
- Physical and environmental description is compressed to functional detail — enough to place the reader, not enough to dwell.
- Chapter endings always raise the next question — the final beat is always a hook forward, not a closing image or reflection.

## CALIBRATION EXAMPLES
**Andy Weir, The Martian** — Every chapter advances Watney's situation and ends with a new problem or decision. Weir's prose is propulsive through compression — the Martian's technical processes are summarized without dwelling, and the interiority is minimal and problem-focused. The reader moves fast because the story moves fast.

**Gillian Flynn, Gone Girl** — The alternating timeline structure is inherently propulsive — each chapter ends with information that makes the reader need the next. Flynn's scene construction is tight: she opens scenes at the action, cuts dialogue as soon as the exchange is complete, and ends on beats that generate forward motion.

**James Patterson, any cross novel** — Patterson's chapter structure (frequently under 10 pages) is a formal commitment to propulsive pacing: chapters exist to deliver a single beat and raise the next question. This is the propulsive technique operating without counterbalance, which is instructive about both the technique's strengths and limitations.

**NEAR-MISS — Lee Child, Make Me** — Child's late-series Reacher novels attempt propulsive pacing but Reacher's interior voice has become extensive enough to slow the momentum — the protagonist's assessments of situations are detailed and careful, which produces a character study rhythm inside what should be propulsive structure. The near-miss failure is that the interiority is no longer in service of decisions but is itself a form of dwelling, converting propulsive to slow-burn at the chapter level.

## FAILURE MODES
- Scenes open with establishing material (location description, character mood setting) rather than at the event itself.
- Dialogue runs past the point of its essential exchange, allowing characters to reflect on what was said.
- Chapters end on reflection or atmosphere rather than on the next question or problem.
- Interiority is given full scenes rather than being embedded in action — the protagonist sits somewhere and thinks.
- Transitions are dramatized rather than summarized — travel, preparation, and recovery are given scene space.

## AI WRITING DEFAULTS TO AVOID
The writing system will produce establishing shots at the start of scenes — physical description that places the reader before the scene begins → Establishing shots stop momentum before it starts → Open on action or dialogue; place the reader in the scene through what is happening, not where they are.

The writing system will produce post-dialogue reflection — characters sit with what was said before the scene moves → Post-dialogue reflection converts propulsive to slow-burn at the beat level → Cut everything after the dialogue's essential exchange; the next beat begins immediately.

The writing system will produce chapter endings on atmosphere or image — a closing sentence that is evocative rather than forward-pointing → Atmospheric chapter endings produce closure; propulsive chapter endings produce the next question → Every chapter must end on a beat that cannot be the last thing that happens.

## CONFLICT WITH OTHER DIMENSIONS

- Conflicts with: pacing-slow-burn
- What creates the friction: Slow-burn requires time in scenes that does not advance events; propulsive requires events to advance constantly.
- Default winner: pacing-propulsive — momentum is either present or absent; a chapter cannot simultaneously give the reader time to dwell and keep them moving.
- Override condition: pacing-slow-burn wins when the story has entered a section where accumulation is what is being built — the propulsive story has earned a slow-burn sequence as preparation for the next event.

- Conflicts with: pacing-procedural
- What creates the friction: Procedural requires scene logic to govern; propulsive requires event logic to govern.
- Default winner: pacing-propulsive — propulsive structure cuts procedural detail when it does not advance events.
- Override condition: pacing-procedural wins when the procedure itself is so interesting and specific that it provides the momentum — the procedure IS propulsive.

- Conflicts with: moral-register-tragic
- What creates the friction: Tragic requires dwell time with the protagonist's virtues before the world turns them against them — the accumulation that makes the destruction meaningful requires time that propulsive pacing will not give.
- Default winner: moral-register-tragic — the moral register requires structural accommodation; tragic structure cannot be executed at propulsive speed.
- Override condition: pacing-propulsive wins in the final act of a tragic story, after the accumulation is complete — the destruction arrives at propulsive speed because the story has already built what it needed.
