## FILE ID
plot-engine-survival

## DIMENSION TYPE
Plot Engine

## ONE-LINE DEFINITION
The story is driven by the protagonist's need to stay alive, intact, or free against an active and credible opposing force.

## FUNCTIONAL DEFINITION
Survival plot engine makes physical, psychological, or social survival the organizing principle of plot. Every scene advances either the threat (the opposing force becomes more capable or better positioned) or the protagonist's capacity to resist it (they gain resources, knowledge, or positioning). The engine fails when scenes produce neither.

Survival is distinct from pursuit: pursuit is a relationship between two specific parties. Survival can include pursuit, but can also include environmental threat, systemic threat, or the accumulated weight of damage over time. The protagonist may not know who is hunting them; they may not be hunted at all but simply exposed to forces that will destroy them unless they find a way to survive.

At the structural level, survival engine organizes around resource depletion — the protagonist begins with certain assets (physical capability, allies, money, information, time) and each scene costs something. The question is whether they can acquire new resources faster than the existing ones deplete.

## READER PSYCHOLOGY
Survival engine gives readers the physiological experience of vicarious danger — the reading experience where the reader's body responds to the protagonist's peril. This is the specific offer of the thriller genre at its most elemental. What it withholds is intellectual engagement as a primary value; survival engine prioritizes physical stakes over epistemic ones.

## PROSE SIGNATURE
- Physical capability and limitation are tracked and consistent — injuries accumulate, exhaustion is real, resources deplete.
- Threat assessment is a constant protagonist activity — the reader is always given the protagonist's current assessment of their position.
- Chapters end at the moment when the protagonist's situation has changed — better or worse or differently — not at a moment of stasis.
- Environmental details are threat-indexed — the physical world is described in terms of what it means for survival.

## CALIBRATION EXAMPLES
**Cormac McCarthy, The Road** — The survival engine is relentless: every chapter depletes the man and boy's resources while advancing the threats against them. McCarthy's prose tracks their physical state with clinical precision — what they eat, how far they walk, how cold they are — because survival is about the body.

**Richard Connell, The Most Dangerous Game** — The survival engine in compressed form: Rainsford's resources and options narrow with each scene while Zaroff's capability increases. The engine's mechanics are visible because the story is short enough to track each resource change.

## FAILURE MODES
- Resources fail to deplete — the protagonist retains full capability throughout, removing the survival calculation.
- The threat is not credible — the protagonist is in less danger than the story claims.
- Survival is achieved through luck or rescue rather than through the protagonist's own adaptive capability.

## AI WRITING DEFAULTS TO AVOID
The writing system will produce a protagonist who recovers fully between survival challenges → Full recovery removes the depletion that makes survival a genuine engine → Damage accumulates; the protagonist is never fully restored to their starting condition.

The writing system will produce threats that fail to advance → Static threat removes the sense that the situation is getting more dangerous → The threat must be specifically more capable or better positioned after each scene than before.

## CONFLICT WITH OTHER DIMENSIONS

- Conflicts with: pacing-slow-burn
- What creates the friction: Survival engine requires the threat to advance; slow-burn withholds advancement.
- Default winner: plot-engine-survival — the engine's requirement for threat advancement overrides slow-burn's preference for accumulation.
- Override condition: pacing-slow-burn wins in sequences where the survival pressure is specifically about accumulating damage over time rather than active threat advancement.

- Conflicts with: moral-register-tragic
- What creates the friction: Tragic requires the protagonist's virtues to produce their destruction; survival engine is indifferent to virtue.
- Default winner: plot-engine-survival — survival governs moment-to-moment plot; tragic governs the story's ultimate shape.
- Override condition: moral-register-tragic wins when the protagonist's survival choices require them to violate their values in ways that produce the tragic destruction.

## COMBINATION NOTES

- Combines with: pacing-thriller-escalation
- Emergent effect: Survival stakes and escalating pressure compound — the protagonist's options narrow as their physical survival becomes simultaneously more necessary and less assured.

- Combines with: moral-register-clear-stakes
- Emergent effect: Survival choices carry moral weight — the protagonist isn't just trying to survive but choosing what they will do to survive, and the moral architecture makes each survival choice legible as a moral decision.
