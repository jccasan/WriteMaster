CHARACTER NAME BLACKLIST — AI-COMMON NAMES TO AVOID

These names appear at disproportionately high rates in AI-generated fiction. Flag any character whose name (first or last) exactly matches this list unless the name was explicitly provided in the author's brain dump.

## DETECTION PROCEDURE

For each named character in the dossier:
1. Extract full name and role
2. Check first name and last name independently against the lists above
3. Flag any exact match (case-insensitive)
4. Exception: if the name appears verbatim in the author's brain dump, do NOT flag it — the author chose it intentionally

## NAME REPLACEMENT GUIDANCE

For each flagged name, propose 3 alternatives that:
- Fit the character's cultural background and setting
- Are pronounceable and memorable without being generic
- Don't appear on this list
- Feel specific to this character's role and arc — a corrupt senator shouldn't share name texture with a street thief

Justify each alternative in 1 sentence: why does this name work for this specific character?

---

## WHAT MAKES A NAME AI-OBVIOUS

Names feel AI-generated when they:
- Sound like fantasy archetypes regardless of genre (Thorn, Vayne, Lyra)
- Are statistically common in training data (Nora, Marcus, Ethan in every genre)
- Are exotic-sounding for no cultural reason (Seraphina as a contemporary suburban character)
- Are thematically on-the-nose (a villain named Drake, a wise woman named Seraphina)
- Combine two generically "strong" sounds with no cultural specificity (Caden, Rylan, Talon)

Strong names are culturally grounded, have texture specific to the character's world, and don't announce the character's archetype before the reader meets them.
